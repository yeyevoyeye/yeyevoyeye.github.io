Reinterpreting Historical Tea Culture through Generative AI

| CMU | Course project of Architectural Intelligence | 2025 |
| Fuser | ComfyUI | Flux | 3D printer |

The project investigates the application of text- and image-conditioned generative models, together with custom LoRA training, as a workflow for cultural reinterpretation and design exploration. Multiple AI platforms, including Fuser, ComfyUI, and Flux, were employed, and the workflow concludes with a 2D-to-3D generation process that produces a physical 3D-printed artifact.

The project originates from a traditional tea-snack practice of the Song Dynasty in China, characterized by social gatherings centered on tea preparation and shared food. A real photograph of a tea table was used as the initial visual input and reinterpreted through generative models to produce still-life compositions referencing classical painting aesthetics. Parametric object compositions designed in Rhino were introduced to guide spatial organization and maintain visual coherence.

An emergent animal-like teapot generated during the process became a focal design element. This artifact was systematically iterated into a series of horse-like teapots, which were used to train a LoRA model for consistent form generation. The project further extends the tea-gathering scene into a cinematic fantasy environment inspired by Elden Ring, transforming a peaceful ritual into a contrasting, high-tension narrative.
